FAQ

Common
questions

Everything you need to know before getting started with ATLAS.Pave.

How long does a survey take?

Surveys typically run at 20–30 km/h. A 10 km corridor can be completed in under an hour of drive time. Processing and report delivery follows within hours of survey completion.

Do we need to close the road?

No. The AiOT device operates from a normal survey vehicle in active traffic. There is no requirement to close, restrict, or cone the road.

Is the PCI output accepted by road authorities?

Yes. ATLAS.Pave computes PCI strictly per ASTM D6433-07 — the same internationally recognised standard used for manual inspections — producing fully auditable, defensible results.

Can you survey both asphalt and concrete roads?

Yes. The AI model detects and classifies distress types for both AC (asphalt concrete) and PCC (Portland cement concrete) surfaces, with separate analysis tables in all reports.

What is the pricing structure?

Pricing is scoped per project based on network size, number of lanes, survey frequency, and required deliverables. Contact us for a tailored proposal — all quotes are obligation-free.

Do you work outside Singapore?

Yes. PAGI Initiative has active projects across Singapore, Indonesia, Malaysia, Germany, and the UAE. We are equipped for international deployment with local coordination support.
